Restarts are not exceptions

4 July 2026 · 9 minute read

In most languages, signaling a problem and abandoning the work are the same act. throw is a jump: by the time anyone with enough context to decide something useful has caught the exception, the stack that knew how to fix it is gone. The catch site gets a corpse and a stack trace.

Common Lisp splits this into three roles. Code that detects a problem signals a condition. Code that knows how to recover publishes restarts, named repairs that remain live on the stack. Code with the authority to decide installs a handler, looks at the menu, and picks one. Nothing unwinds until somebody chooses to unwind. 

;;; The payment layer knows two sensible repairs.
(define-condition gateway-timeout (error)
  ((endpoint :initarg :endpoint :reader endpoint))
  (:report (lambda (condition stream)
             (format stream "Gateway timed out talking to ~A"
                     (endpoint condition)))))

(defun charge (order processor)
  "Attempt to charge ORDER, offering recovery to whoever is above us."
  (restart-case (submit-payment order processor)
    (use-backup-processor ()
      :report "Retry the charge through the backup processor."
      (submit-payment order *backup-processor*))
    (queue-for-review ()
      :report "Park the order for a human."
      (queue-order order :reason 'payment-review))))

The batch job three frames up is the code with authority. It wants throughput, not heroics, so it picks the backup processor and keeps going. Note what does not happen: the loop does not re-enter the payment layer, no work is redone, and charge never learns who made the call. 

;; Policy lives at the top; mechanism stays below.
(defun charge-batch (orders)
  (handler-bind ((gateway-timeout
                   (lambda (condition)
                     (declare (ignore condition))
                     (invoke-restart 'use-backup-processor))))
    (mapcar (lambda (order)
              (charge order *primary-processor*))
            orders)))

And when no handler exists at all, the debugger presents the same restart menu to you, interactively, with the stack still warm. The recovery strategy you prototype at the REPL on Tuesday is the handler-bind you commit on Wednesday. That is the part exceptions never gave us: the error path is a first-class interface, designed where the knowledge lives, chosen where the authority lives.
