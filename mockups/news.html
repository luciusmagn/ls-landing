Valley fiber cooperative lights its forty-thousandth home

By Marta Kolek, infrastructure desk

The Elbe Valley fiber cooperative connected its forty-thousandth household on Friday, eleven years after a village meeting voted to trench the first three kilometers of duct itself. The cooperative now passes more premises than the regional incumbent, and it did so without a single tower crane or a national advertising budget.

The model is deliberately boring. Members pay a one-time share, the cooperative buys access to municipal duct where it exists and digs where it does not, and every build is published as an itemized ledger, machine time, splice counts, and coffee included. "People forgive a delay they can read about," said board chair Alena Prochová. "They do not forgive a mystery."

The milestone connection, a retired schoolteacher's cottage in Třebušín, was spliced by the same two-person crew that lit the cooperative's first customer in 2015. Symmetric gigabit service remains at the founding price.

Rail operator opens its timetable feed, and the apps arrive by dinner

By Ondřej Rys, systems desk

The national rail operator switched its long-promised timetable feed to public access at 09:00 on Thursday. By 18:00, volunteers had shipped four departure boards, a delay heatmap, and a command-line client. The operator's own app team says it is "watching with interest," which former employees describe as the department's highest known form of praise.

The feed publishes plain, versioned files over HTTP with a documented schema, a decision the operator credits to one stubborn archivist in its data office. No developer portal, no API keys, no quarterly deprecations. Just files, on time, like the trains aspire to be.

University lab prints spare parts for a 1984 pen plotter, publishes the whole recipe

By Jana Šimková, science desk

A materials lab has reverse-engineered the sprung carriage of a 1984 flatbed plotter after the last known spare cracked during a student demonstration. The lab published measured drawings, tolerances, and failed attempts alongside the final part, on the argument that the failures are the useful literature.

The plotter is back on the department's hallway roster, where it draws one visitor's name per day in a steady 0.3 millimeter line. Sign-up is a clipboard.
