## Design Context

### Users

Lambda Symbolics speaks to technically curious programmers, prospective
students, and people evaluating its software. They arrive to understand what
has been built, inspect concrete evidence, and reach the relevant product,
course, source repository, or contact point without passing through a sales
funnel.

### Brand Personality

Rigorous, literary, and quietly strange. The site should feel trustworthy and
human, with the confidence of a well-edited technical manual and enough wit to
reward close reading. It should create curiosity without hype.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the Interlisp Paper language: black ink on one warm off-white substrate,
Times New Roman for prose, Nimbus Roman Mono for code, sharp one-pixel rules,
and sparse bitmap dithering for depth. The visual reference is a programmable
paper document or an old workstation manual rendered with modern typographic
care. Do not introduce flat gray, rounded corners, gradients, glossy effects,
generic card grids, or decorative JavaScript. Page-specific character should
come from information architecture, specimens, tables, transcripts, and
precise writing rather than a new visual theme.

### Design Principles

1. Show evidence early. Prefer real commands, properties, schedules, and
   examples over abstract promises.
2. Keep one paper substrate. Establish hierarchy through type, rules, spacing,
   inversion, and dither rather than additional surfaces.
3. Make the unusual legible. Pair unfamiliar ideas with plain explanations and
   a direct next action.
4. Preserve document semantics. Use headings, tables, definition lists,
   transcripts, skip links, visible focus, and layouts that remain complete on
   small screens and in print.
5. Stay serious without becoming sterile. A precise phrase or unexpected
   juxtaposition may carry personality, but never at the expense of accuracy.
